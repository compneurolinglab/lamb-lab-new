Research

Our interdisciplinary research program advances computational neurolinguistics by combining insights from linguistics, computer science, and neuroscience to understand how the brain processes language

Core Research Areas

We investigate language processing across multiple levels of analysis, from neural mechanisms to computational models, advancing both theoretical understanding and practical applications

Neural Language Processing

We investigate how the human brain processes language using computational models and advanced neuroimaging techniques. Our groundbreaking research on scaling demonstrates how large language models align with brain processing during language comprehension, providing insights into both artificial and biological language systems.

View Publications

Computational Models of Meaning

Our lab develops sophisticated computational frameworks to understand how meaning is composed and processed in both artificial intelligence systems and the human brain. We investigate semantic representation, contextual meaning construction, and compositional semantics through mathematical modeling and empirical validation.

Learn More

Neuroimaging & Brain Studies

Using state-of-the-art neuroimaging techniques including fMRI, MEG, and EEG, we study the neural mechanisms underlying language comprehension and production. Our research includes cross-linguistic studies and comparative analyses across species to understand the evolutionary foundations of language.

Explore Methods

Naturalistic Language Datasets

We create and analyze comprehensive datasets for studying language processing in naturalistic contexts. Our Le Petit Prince Multi-talker corpus provides 7T fMRI and EEG data for understanding speech comprehension in realistic listening conditions, supporting reproducible neurolinguistics research.

Access Data

AI-Brain Alignment Studies

Our research examines how artificial intelligence models, particularly large language models, align with human brain activity during language processing. We study the relationship between model scaling, instruction tuning, and neural alignment to advance both AI and neuroscience.

Read Papers

Cross-Species Language Studies

We conduct comparative studies examining language-related processing across species to understand the evolutionary foundations of human language capabilities. Our research investigates acoustic pattern learning, vocalization processing, and neural mechanisms shared across mammals.

Featured Research Projects

Highlighting our most impactful ongoing and recently completed research initiatives

Language Model Scaling and Brain Alignment

This project investigates how scaling properties of large language models relate to their alignment with human brain activity during language processing. Published in Nature Computational Science, our findings show that scaling, but not instruction tuning, increases model-brain correspondence, with implications for both AI development and neuroscience.

Key Findings: Model size correlates with neural alignment; instruction tuning doesn't improve brain correspondence; specific neural networks show stronger alignment patterns.

View Publication

Le Petit Prince Multi-talker Dataset

A comprehensive naturalistic language processing dataset featuring 7T fMRI and EEG recordings while participants listen to Le Petit Prince stories. Published in Scientific Data, this resource enables research on speech comprehension, multi-talker processing, and cross-linguistic comparisons in ecologically valid conditions.

Features: High-resolution 7T fMRI; synchronized EEG; multiple talkers; multilingual content; open access for research community.

Access Dataset

Neural State Space Modeling

We model the trajectory of language processing in neural state space, tracking how brain representations evolve during sentence comprehension. This work, presented at SNL 2024, reveals dynamic patterns of neural activity that correspond to different stages of linguistic processing from phonemes to meaning.

Innovations: Dynamic neural tracking; state space analysis; temporal modeling of language comprehension; cross-modal neural integration.

Research Methods & Approaches

Our interdisciplinary methodology combines cutting-edge techniques from multiple fields

Neuroimaging Techniques

High-resolution 7T fMRI, MEG, and EEG to capture neural activity during language processing with excellent spatial and temporal resolution.

Computational Modeling

Advanced machine learning models, neural networks, and mathematical frameworks to simulate and understand language processing mechanisms.

Behavioral Experiments

Carefully designed psycholinguistic experiments to test specific hypotheses about language comprehension and production in controlled conditions.

Cross-linguistic Analysis

Comparative studies across different languages and cultures to understand universal versus language-specific processing mechanisms.

Big Data Analysis

Large-scale analysis of naturalistic language datasets, corpus linguistics, and computational approaches to language pattern discovery.

AI Model Evaluation

Systematic comparison of artificial intelligence language models with human brain activity to understand alignment and differences.

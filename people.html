Our Team

Meet the researchers advancing computational neurolinguistics and bridging the gap between artificial intelligence and human language processing

Principal Investigator

Dr. Jixing Li

Assistant Professor, Principal Investigator

Dr. Li directs the LAnguages, Machines & Brains Lab and leads interdisciplinary research at the intersection of Linguistics, Natural Language Processing, and Cognitive Neuroscience. His work focuses on understanding how the human brain processes language through computational modeling and neuroimaging techniques. His research has been published in top-tier venues including Nature Computational Science and Scientific Data, advancing our understanding of the neural basis of language comprehension and the alignment between artificial intelligence and human cognition.

Ph.D. Students

Zhengwu

Ph.D. Student

Research focus on modeling the trajectory of language processing in neural state space. Presented work at the Society for the Neurobiology of Language (SNL) 2024 conference in Brisbane, Australia. Currently investigating how neural representations of language evolve during sentence comprehension using computational models and neuroimaging techniques.

Chengcheng

Ph.D. Student

Working on functional specificity in multimodal large language models and the human brain. Presented research at SNL 2024 on computational approaches to understanding brain-language relationships. Investigating how AI models can inform our understanding of human language processing through systematic lesioning studies and computational modeling approaches.

Qian Wang

Ph.D. Student

Research interests in computational neurolinguistics and neural language processing. Contributing to lab's work on meaning composition and large language model alignment with brain processing. Focusing on semantic representation and computational modeling of linguistic meaning, with particular interest in how context shapes meaning construction in the brain.

Qimiao Qiu

Ph.D. Student

Investigating speech comprehension and multi-talker processing using computational models and neuroimaging techniques. Working on naturalistic language processing datasets including the Le Petit Prince multilingual corpus. Specializing in auditory scene analysis and speech perception in complex acoustic environments, with applications to hearing aid design.

Weiqiong Zhao

Ph.D. Student

Research focus on associative encoding of speech sounds and computational modeling of auditory processing in both human and animal models. Contributing to cross-species language research that bridges neuroscience and linguistics. Investigating neural mechanisms of acoustic pattern learning and their relationship to language acquisition across different species.
